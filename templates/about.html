About the catalogue

The UQ Anthropology Museum houses a wealth of items ranging from everyday implements to diverse performance paraphernalia. There are water-craft, paintings, bodywear, hunting gear, a large quantity of stone tools, including grindstones and axes and a collection of over 6,000 photographs.

Plus the "Notes regarding collection records", "Aboriginal and Torres-Strait Islander Warning", and "Copyright" sections from the homepage"

Catalogue technology

The UQ Anthropology Museum catalogue was developed as a partnership between the eResearch Group within the UQ School of Information Technology and Electrical Engineering and the UQ Anthropology Museum. It uses open source technologies including the Python Django web framework, Apache Solr search server,

The user interface was designed by the UQ Office of Marketing and Communications.

Source code for catalogue is available in GitHub under a BSD open source licence.
